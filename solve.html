Declining Margins at a Telecom Operator

Case Prompt

Your client is TelecomCo, a mid-size mobile network operator in India with a national subscriber base. Over the past 18 months, their EBITDA margin has fallen from 28% to 19% despite overall revenue growing at 6% year-on-year.

The CEO is concerned and has brought your firm in to diagnose the issue and recommend a path to recovery. You have been asked to lead the engagement.

Your task: Structure your approach, identify the likely root causes, and present an action plan that could restore EBITDA margins to the prior level within 24 months.

Difficulty
Opening Structure
Start by splitting the problem: the margin decline can come from (a) revenue falling faster than costs or (b) costs rising faster than revenue — or both. With revenue up 6% but EBITDA margin down ~9 pp, costs are clearly growing disproportionately.
Issue Tree
Revenue Pressure
  • ARPU decline (₹182 → ₹171) from competitive pricing
  • Mix shift: prepaid growing vs. postpaid/enterprise
  • Data monetisation lag despite 5G rollout
Cost Escalation
  • 5G capex → higher depreciation + spectrum cost
  • Network Opex increase (energy, maintenance)
  • Marketing spend to defend market share
  • Debt servicing cost if capex was financed
Margin Math Check
Revenue = ₹8,400 Cr. At 28%: EBITDA = ₹2,352 Cr. At 19%: EBITDA = ₹1,596 Cr. Gap = ₹756 Cr. The 5G capex of ₹2,100 Cr over 18 months → roughly ₹140 Cr/month in additional depreciation if amortised over 5 years. That alone explains ~₹280 Cr/year, pointing to a structural cost shift — not just a one-time item.
Root Cause Synthesis
  • Primary: 5G capex has raised fixed cost base before monetisation — a classic over-investment-ahead-of-revenue timing issue
  • Secondary: Price war from Jio compressed ARPU, shrinking margin on every subscriber
  • Compounding: Market share defence spend (SG&A) added to Opex at the same time
Recommendations (24-month path)
  • Revenue lever: Bundle 5G plans with premium tiers — convert data usage into ARPU recovery (target ₹190+ ARPU within 12 months)
  • Cost lever: Passive infra sharing with other operators to reduce network Opex 15–20%
  • Portfolio lever: Double down on B2B/enterprise 5G use cases where margins are structurally higher
  • Short-term: Pause non-critical capex; free cash for operating debt reduction
Interviewer signal: Strong candidates identify the 5G timing mismatch in the first 2 minutes. Elite candidates also quantify the ARPU-contribution gap and flag the B2B monetisation angle unprompted.

Interview Tips
Always clarify: is the margin decline EBITDA or PAT? 5G depreciation affects PAT more than EBITDA.
Ask if the trend is uniform across all 4 circles — a concentrated decline points to a regional factor.
Revenue growing + margin falling = costs are the primary story. Lead with costs.
